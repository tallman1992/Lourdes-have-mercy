Lourdes Have Mercy

World Cup Round 1 recap

Story by Matt Rickard

In this town known for its miracles, the only holy water in sight was falling from the sky to create one of the rowdiest opening World Cup rounds we've seen in years.

Though the track remained completely unchanged from its debute last season, the conditions made it anything but the same. With rain falling heavily each night and intermittently through the day, the track was in a constant state of flux.

With each passing run things changed and just as riders began to feel comfortbale, or things began to dry up, the heavens opened up to wipe the slate clean and send everyone back to the drawing board.

Just when the riders thought the track would show some mercy, it goes and does the complete oppsoite.

The Setting

Lourdes is a small town situated in the foothills of the Pyranees in South-West France. In 1858 Lourdes rose to prominence in France and abroad due to the Marian apparitions seen by the peasant girl Bernadette Soubirous, who was later canonized.

Shortly thereafter the city became one of the world's most important sites of pilgrimage and religious tourism. Today Lourdes hosts around six million visitors every year from all corners of the world. This constant stream of pilgrims and tourists transformed quiet Lourdes into the second most important center of tourism in France, second only to Paris, and the third most important site of international Catholic pilgrimage after Rome and the Holy Land.

The track

The Lourdes WC track is one of the most technical and challenging on the circuit. Having broken through onto the scene in 2015, the track offers an array of difficult obstacles, pushing riders to their absolute limits. It is for this reason the venue is now a favourite amongst riders.
